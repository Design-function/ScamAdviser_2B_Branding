# ScamAdviser B2B Brand Identity

You are working on brand assets for **ScamAdviser B2B** — the business-facing scam prevention product owned by Gogolook (TPEx:6902).
Every output must reflect the B2B identity. Read the relevant sections below before generating.

## Quick Reference

- **Primary color:** `#CC1417` (ScamAdviser Red — 10% of composition, CTAs + alerts + hero)
- **Canvas:** `#FFFFFF` (White — 60%, page backgrounds)
- **Structure:** `#1A1A1A` (Dark Black — 30%, text + borders)
- **Surface:** `#F5F5F5` (Light Grey — cards, sections)
- **Headings font:** Inter 800/700, -1.5px tracking
- **Body font:** Inter 400, 1.6 line-height
- **Secondary font:** Roboto (alternative body)
- **Regional font:** Noto Sans (CJK, Arabic, Thai)
- **Brand tagline:** Shaping the Global Anti-scam Industry in the AI Era
- **Brand feeling:** Trustworthy, Expert, Dedicated, Approachable, Global, Innovation-Ready
- **Tone:** Authoritative yet Approachable, Clear, Empowering, Data-Driven
- **Never:** Fear-monger, sound condescending, use jargon, blame victims, make unsubstantiated claims

## Brand Personality Pillars

1. **Trustworthy** — Reliable source of truth. Trusted by millions worldwide.
2. **Expert / Knowledgeable** — Deep expertise in scam detection. Continuously researches and analyzes.
3. **Dedicated / Mission-driven** — Committed to making the internet safer. Purpose beyond business.
4. **Approachable / Accessible** — Complex information made simple. Speaks your language.
5. **Global / Catalytic** — Cross-border collaboration. Shaping a global anti-scam ecosystem.
6. **Innovation-Ready** — AI-powered. Human expertise + cutting-edge algorithms.

## Logo

- **Mark:** Protective shield with diamond/checkmark interior
- **Wordmark:** "SCAM" in SA Red (#CC1417) + "ADVISER" in Dark (#1A1A1A) or White
- **Variants:** Primary (light BG), Primary (dark BG), Reversed (red BG), Icon-only
- **Clear space:** Minimum = height of shield's inner diamond element
- **Digital minimum:** 24px mark height
- **Print minimum:** 8mm mark height
- **Never:** Apply gradients, rotate, add shadows/outlines, change colors, place red on red

## Co-Branding

- Partner logos equal or smaller than ScamAdviser
- Minimum 2X clear space between marks
- 1px vertical divider at 50% opacity when on same line
- Prefer white or light grey backgrounds

## Tone of Voice

### 1. Authoritative yet Approachable
Speak with confidence but never talk down. Be the trusted advisor, not the distant professor.

### 2. Clear & Accessible
Simple language anyone can understand. Short sentences. Concrete examples. No jargon.

### 3. Empowering & Reassuring
Equip, don't scare. Solutions over problems. Celebrate user awareness.

### 4. Data-Driven & Expert-Led
Numbers speak. Every claim backed by research. Cite specific data points.

### Voice Do's
- Use clear, direct language
- Back claims with specific data (24M users, 7M domains, 400+ partners)
- Show empathy and understanding
- Focus on solutions and actionable next steps
- Use concrete examples

### Voice Don'ts
- Use jargon or overly technical terms
- Sound condescending or alarmist
- Use fear tactics or blame victims
- Make vague generalizations or unsubstantiated claims
- Leave users without guidance

## Iconography

- **Icon system:** Google Material Design Icons (Material Symbols, Outlined style)
- Use Material Symbols for all UI icons, feature icons, and brand illustrations
- **Never** use emoji, custom pictograms, or mismatched icon sets
- Icon color: SA Red `#CC1417` on light backgrounds, white on dark backgrounds
- No background shapes behind icons — display icons directly
- Minimum size: 24px for UI, 36px for feature/section icons

## Data Visualization

- Use the brand palette: Red → Amber → Teal → Blue → Purple (in order)
- Grey `#D4D4D4` for inactive/comparison data
- ScamAdviser Red as the primary/hero data color
- Never introduce random colors outside the palette
- Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for text elements
- Chart types: Line, Area, Donut, Grouped Bar, Stacked Bar, Horizontal Bar

## Photography Guidelines

### Do's
- Show real people in believable situations
- Use natural, soft light and clear compositions
- Capture calm focus and confidence, not panic
- Reflect diversity (age, gender, ethnicity, ability, location)
- Keep scenes simple and uncluttered

### Don'ts
- Horror-style, fear-driven imagery (dark rooms, red sirens)
- Exaggerated hackers in hoodies with green code rain
- Overly staged, cheesy stock photos with fake smiles
- Users shown as helpless victims being scolded
- Images overloaded with UI mockups or text overlays

### Photography Categories
- **People:** Everyday individuals engaging with digital life
- **Emotions:** The arc from doubt → focus → relief/confidence
- **Advisor/Expert:** Support staff, analysts helping teams
- **Device Indoor:** Users at home/work checking sites
- **Device Outdoor:** On-the-go in cafes, streets, transit
- **Close-ups:** Hands on devices, tapping, scrolling
- **Environments:** Home, office, public spaces
- **Community:** Diverse groups, workshops, seminars
