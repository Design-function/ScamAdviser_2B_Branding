02 — Tone of Voice

How We Speak

Our voice is authoritative yet approachable. We share complex information clearly, empower our audience with knowledge, and always lead with data-driven expertise.

▼

Authoritative yet Approachable

We speak with confidence and expertise, but never talk down to our audience. We are the trusted advisor, not the distant professor.

Do

  • Use clear, direct language
  • Back claims with data
  • Show empathy and understanding

Don't

  • Use jargon or overly technical terms
  • Sound condescending or alarmist
  • Make unsubstantiated claims
▼

Clear & Accessible

We break down complex topics into simple, understandable language. Anyone, regardless of their tech knowledge, should be able to understand our message.

Do

  • Use short sentences
  • Explain technical concepts simply
  • Use concrete examples

Don't

  • Assume prior knowledge
  • Use acronyms without explanation
  • Write long, complex paragraphs
▼

Empowering & Reassuring

We don't scare people — we equip them. Our messaging inspires confidence and gives users the tools they need to protect themselves.

Do

  • Focus on solutions, not just problems
  • Celebrate user awareness
  • Provide actionable next steps

Don't

  • Use fear tactics
  • Blame victims of scams
  • Leave users without guidance
▼

Data-Driven & Expert-Led

We let the numbers speak. Every claim is backed by research, data, and real-world evidence. We are the authority because we have done the work.

Do

  • Cite specific data points
  • Reference trusted sources
  • Show methodology when relevant

Don't

  • Make vague generalizations
  • Present opinions as facts
  • Hide behind buzzwords

Brand Voice in Action

For Partners
Instead of: "We provide anti-scam solutions for enterprise stakeholders."
Write: "Protect your customers with real-time scam intelligence — trusted by 400+ partners globally."
For Businesses
Instead of: "Our platform enables comprehensive threat mitigation."
Write: "Spot scam websites before your customers do. Our API checks 7M+ domains in real time."
For End Users
Instead of: "Exercise caution when interacting with unverified web properties."
Write: "Not sure about that website? Check it on ScamAdviser — it takes 2 seconds."

04 — Co-Branding

Partner Guidelines

When ScamAdviser appears alongside partner brands, consistent spacing and hierarchy ensure both brands maintain their integrity and recognition.

Gogolook Co-Branding

ScamAdviser is a product of Gogolook (TPEx:6902). When both brands appear together, the Gogolook logo appears at the same height or smaller than ScamAdviser. A vertical divider separates the two marks with equal spacing on both sides.

Partner Logo Placement

Partner logos should be placed at equal or smaller size relative to the ScamAdviser logo. Maintain minimum clear space of 2X between marks. Use a vertical divider (1px, 50% opacity) when logos appear on the same line.

Co-Branding Rules

SIZING

Partner logo should never be larger than ScamAdviser. Match visual weight, not exact pixel height.

SPACING

Minimum 2X clear space between logos. Use a 1px vertical divider at 50% opacity when on the same line.

BACKGROUND

Both logos must have sufficient contrast. Prefer white or light grey backgrounds for co-branded materials.

09 — Photography

Visual Storytelling

ScamAdviser's visual storytelling should reflect its mission to protect, inform, and empower users in the digital world. Our photography supports this by being authentic, human-focused, and clear. Never overly staged, fear-driven, or generic.

Our User (People)

Everyday individuals of different ages, backgrounds, and locations engaging with digital life (shopping, banking, messaging, browsing). Authentic, not staged corporate stock.

Visual Style

  • Natural light, soft contrast
  • Candid moments, slight movement
  • Clear focus on faces and body language

Keywords

Authentic Diverse Relatable Engaged Empowered

Emotions

The emotional arc of scams — Before: doubt, concern, confusion. During: focus, checking, verifying. After: relief, confidence, calm empowerment.

Visual Style

  • Close-ups of expressions and hands
  • Soft, calm color environments
  • Composition moves from "tense" to "open"

Keywords

Concern Focus Relief Empowerment Calmness

Advisor & Scam Expert

ScamAdviser as a trusted, human expert: support staff, analysts, partners, or educators helping users or teams. Supports "Advisor / Scam Expert", "Trustworthy", "Dedicated".

Device Usage — Indoor

Users on phones, laptops, or tablets at home or work using ScamAdviser to check a site, read an alert, or learn about scams.

Device Usage — Outdoor

People using ScamAdviser "on the go": in a cafe, park, train, street, or while traveling.

Device Interaction Close-up

Tight shots of hands and devices: tapping, scrolling, reading scam alerts, seeing trust scores.

Context & Environments

Three sub-contexts: Home & Everyday Life, Work & Professional, and Public / On-the-go.

Global & Community

People from different countries, communities, and age groups; group learning; events about scams. Reflects the "Global" and "Catalytic" brand traits.

Photography Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do's

  • Show real people in believable situations
  • Use natural, soft light and clear compositions
  • Capture calm focus and confidence, not panic
  • Reflect diversity (age, gender, ethnicity, ability, location)
  • Keep scenes simple and uncluttered, easy to read

✗ Don'ts

  • Don't use horror-style, fear-driven imagery (dark rooms, red sirens)
  • Don't show exaggerated hackers in hoodies and green code rain
  • Don't use overly staged, cheesy stock photos with fake smiles
  • Don't show users as helpless victims being scolded or embarrassed
  • Don't overload images with UI mockups, text, or infographic overlays
